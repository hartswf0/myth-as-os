Core Concepts

Definitions, origins in wrestling, and political applications.

Kayfabe

Definition

System of layered falsehoods maintained by mutual agreement between performers and audience

Wrestling Origin

Agreement not to reveal matches are predetermined

Political Application

Maintaining illusion of meaningful choice between pre-approved candidates

Kayfabrication

Definition

The process by which real systems transition to fake but more manageable versions

Wrestling Origin

Transition from dangerous real wrestling to safer scripted entertainment

Political Application

Converting genuine democracy into managed spectacle

GIN (Gated Institutional Narrative)

Definition

Protective narrative framework that gates institutional discourse

Wrestling Origin

Storylines and character development that frame matches

Political Application

Official narratives that shape acceptable political discourse

DISC (Distributed Idea Suppression Complex)

Definition

Emergent system that suppresses disruptive ideas to protect institutional fragility

Wrestling Origin

Preventing fans from learning behind-the-scenes reality

Political Application

Media blackouts, strategic silence, algorithmic suppression of dissent

Stratified Lies

Definition

Hierarchical system of deceptions operating at multiple levels simultaneously

Wrestling Origin

Multiple levels: in-ring, backstage, media interviews, personal life

Political Application

Public rhetoric, party positioning, donor interests, deep state coordination

Work vs Shoot

Definition

Work = scripted/fake performance; Shoot = genuine/real occurrence

Wrestling Origin

Distinguishing real incidents from planned storylines

Political Application

Real crises vs manufactured controversies for political advantage

Breaking Kayfabe

Definition

Revealing the constructed nature of the performance, destroying its effectiveness

Wrestling Origin

Wrestlers appearing out of character or revealing script

Political Application

Revealing how political theater actually operates

Examples Across Domains

Mechanisms, audience participation, benefits/costs, and current status.

Professional Wrestling

Mechanism

Predetermined outcomes presented as competitive sport

Audience

Fans know it's scripted but emotionally invest in storylines

Benefits

Safer than real combat, more entertaining than boring matches

Costs When Exposed

Loss of emotional investment, decline in viewership

Status: Post-kayfabe era - openly acknowledged as entertainment

Electoral Politics

Mechanism

"Magician's choice" - all candidates acceptable to international order

Audience

Voters know politicians lie but still engage in electoral theater

Benefits

Prevents populist disruption of international agreements

Costs When Exposed

Political instability, questioning of legitimate authority

Status: Kayfabe breaking down - rise of populism, outsider candidates

Financial Markets

Mechanism

Market "fundamentals" divorced from actual price movements

Audience

Investors know markets are manipulated but follow "analysis"

Benefits

Prevents market crashes from revealing underlying instability

Costs When Exposed

Market crashes when reality intrudes (2008, etc.)

Status: Increasing awareness of Fed manipulation, Bitcoin alternative

Media/Journalism

Mechanism

Gatekeeper role determining what stories get coverage

Audience

Readers know media bias exists but consume preferred narratives

Benefits

Maintains advertiser-friendly content, avoids controversy

Costs When Exposed

Loss of credibility, audience fragmentation

Status: Legacy media losing gatekeeper power to social media

Academic/Science

Mechanism

Peer review system protecting established paradigms from disruption

Audience

Academics know grant/publication games but play along

Benefits

Preserves research funding streams, prevents paradigm chaos

Costs When Exposed

Scientific stagnation, loss of public trust in expertise

Status: Replication crisis exposing flawed peer review system

Corporate Culture

Mechanism

Corporate mission statements vs actual profit maximization

Audience

Employees know corporate culture is performance but participate

Benefits

Maintains productivity through shared purpose illusion

Costs When Exposed

Employee cynicism, reduced organizational effectiveness

International Relations
